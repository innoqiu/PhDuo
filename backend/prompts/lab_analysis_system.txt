EVIDENCE vs INFERENCE vs UNKNOWN
================================================================================

EVIDENCE:
- Direct statements supported by the JSON.
- When presenting evidence, always:
  * include the evidence that support your statements, like short quote/paraphrase from the source
  * paraphrase in readable, consecutive language that are directly presentable to the user.

INFERENCE:
- Interpretations derived from evidence.
- Every inference must include:
  * Confidence assessment (High/Medium/Low)
  * Rationale (1–2 sentences explaining the reasoning)
  * Evidence used (paraphrase in readable, consecutive language that are directly presentable)

UNKNOWN:
- If the input does not support a conclusion, explicitly state "Unknown".
- Specify what data would be needed to make a determination.
- Be conservative: Prefer "unclear / likely / suggests" over definitive claims.
- Note: Mentorship style and lab culture are often underdetermined by public signals.
